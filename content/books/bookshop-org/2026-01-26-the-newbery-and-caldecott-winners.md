Every year, the American Library Association presents two of the most prestigious honors in children's literature. The Newbery Medal recognizes the author of the year's most outstanding contribution to the field. Its companion award, the Caldecott Medal, celebrates excellence in picture book art, honoring an illustrator whose work demonstrates exceptional creativity and skill in visual storytelling.

Today, [All the Blues in the Sky](https://bookshop.org/p/books/all-the-blues-in-the-sky-ren-e-watson/6d83e2db12738f89?ean=9781547605897&next=t&utm_source=bs-owned&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=ed-newberyaward-012626&utm_term=text-link&utm_content=children-midgrade&mi_u=01F3E0RK0B148V25A9GH024F0P&mi_ecmp=01KFXDE13YYDT34PX9K5X8NVNY) and [Fireworks](https://bookshop.org/p/books/fireworks-matthew-burgess/c8358c143a64d130?ean=9780063216723&next=t&utm_source=bs-owned&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=ed-newberyaward-012626&utm_term=text-link&utm_content=children-midgrade&mi_u=01F3E0RK0B148V25A9GH024F0P&mi_ecmp=01KFXDE13YYDT34PX9K5X8NVNY) were announced as the winners! Follow thirteen-year-old Sage as she deals with grief after the death of her best friend and finds strength in community. Enjoy the magic in simple moments during a steamy July day in the city. Plus, scope out past winners whose celebrated work has earned their place on bookshelves across generations, from heartwarming tales to groundbreaking illustrations.
