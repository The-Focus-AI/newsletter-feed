# Designing My Own Graphic Design Workshop: On Mental Gymnastics, Inspiration, and Finding Joy

**From:** Beth Mathews <bethmathews@substack.com>
**Date:** Tue, 20 Jan 2026 22:38:29 +0000

---

View this post on the web at https://bethmathews.substack.com/p/designing-my-own-graphic-design-workshop

Greetings, friends!
I’ve been chatting in this space for a while about hosting my first in-person graphic design workshop, and I’m happy to report that I did it! This past weekend, I hosted 8 designers in a lovely space  in Los Angeles for a 2-day workshop, teaching them to create vintage-inspired graphics through storytelling and digital design. And let me tell you, the designers who signed up were so, so sweet and talented. I was truly in awe of how amazing these 8 humans were and felt so lucky that they chose to invest in this workshop and spend their weekend with me.
Planning, creating the curriculum, designing a 200+ page presentation deck, hosting, and then actually teaching stretched me in ways that I haven’t experienced in a long time. There were times when my brain physically hurt as I tried to map out all the pieces of how to explain my design process in a concise and (hopefully) engaging way. There were several sleepless nights, worrying, “Can I actually do this?!” I became really acquainted with the local library, as I found the quiet rooms super helpful for sorting through the mental maze of finalizing the pieces together. 
Why did I want to host a design workshop?
