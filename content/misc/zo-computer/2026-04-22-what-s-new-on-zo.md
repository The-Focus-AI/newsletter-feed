🚢 What’s new
Connect Zo to your laptop, PC, or Mac Mini
Zo can now access files, operate the browser, and control apps on devices you own. Simply install the desktop app , and enable the Devices  feature in Settings. Zo can control your browser, use iMessage, access Apple Notes and Obsidian, or run Terminal commands.
Chat with Zo from Discord & Slack
You can now chat with Zo in Discord. Go to Settings > Channels to connect Zo to your Discord account and add Discord servers.
You can also chat with Zo in Slack. Add the Zo app to your Slack workspace, and then DM or mention @Zo to connect your account. 
Publish files and folders
Zo can now publish files & folders , hosted on your personal cloud. Just ask Zo:
Publish this folder to zo.pub
Give me a link to share these files
Make this folder public
More control over Zo’s tools
You can now control which tools Zo has access to. Go to Settings > AI > Personas, select a Persona, and edit the permissions for any of your Zo Personas. When you message that Persona, it will only have the tools that you allow.
Native X integration, localization, and what’s next
Zo can now connect directly to your X account to post and respond to DMs. You can combine this with Zo’s X Search  tool to fully automate your presence on X.
You can change your Zo’s interface language in Settings > UX. Let us know  if you have feedback on any of our translations, or want to request another language.
