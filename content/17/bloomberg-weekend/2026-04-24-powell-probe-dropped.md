The unprecedented criminal investigation by the Trump administration into Fed 
Chair Jerome Powellwas abruptly called off Friday 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-24/doj-drops-probe-into-powell-and-fed-building-costs?cmpid=eveus&utm_campaign=eveus&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260424&utm_content=3839>
, potentially clearing the way for his proposed replacement, Kevin Warsh, to 
win Senate approval. A key Republican senator, Thom Tillis of North Carolina, 
vowed to blockWarsh’s confirmation 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-23/fed-chair-nominee-kevin-warsh-says-he-would-end-forward-guidance?cmpid=eveus&utm_campaign=eveus&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260424&utm_content=3839>
 unless the matter was dropped.

The probe, ostensibly about construction cost overruns, has been condemned by 
critics of the administration and others as an example of the weaponization of 
the Justice Department against Donald Trump’s perceived enemies. In this case, 
the president’s very public desire for the Fed to cut rates in the hopes it 
will boost the economy was seen as the reason behind the investigation. A 
federal judge recently alluded to the contention,excoriating prosecutors 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-13/judge-rejects-doj-subpoenas-of-fed-board-in-powell-case?cmpid=eveus&utm_campaign=eveus&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260424&utm_content=3839>
 as he refused to let them issue subpoenas.
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-24/doj-drops-probe-into-powell-and-fed-building-costs>
Jeanine PirroPhotographer: Yuri Gripas/Abaca/Bloomberg
For his part, Powell vowed to remain at the Fed in his role as a member of its 
Board of Governors—a term that runs to 2028—until the probe was resolved “with
transparency and finality 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-18/powell-says-no-intention-of-leaving-fed-until-investigation-over?cmpid=eveus&utm_campaign=eveus&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260424&utm_content=3839>
.” The Trump administration announcement, however, may not be enough for Powell 
to leave the Fed, said Derek Tang of Monetary Policy Analytics. “I am unsure it 
meets Powell’s ‘finality’ precondition to leave the Fed. After all, what’s to 
stop a probe from coming up again, once the White House gets Warsh confirmed?”

US Senator Elizabeth Warren appeared to agree, pointing to US Attorney Jeanine 
Pirro’s pledge to possibly resume the Powell probe at a later date, as well as 
a Trump administration investigation of another Fed governor.

“Let’s be clear what the Justice Department announced today,” the Democrat 
said. “They threatened to restart the bogus criminal investigation into Fed 
Chair Powell at any time while failing to drop theirridiculous criminal probe 
against Governor Lisa Cook 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-21/fed-independence-is-in-play-when-supreme-court-hears-lisa-cook-case?cmpid=eveus&utm_campaign=eveus&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260424&utm_content=3839>
. Anyone who believes Donald Trump’s corrupt scheme to take over the Fed is 
over is fooling themselves.”

—David E. Rovella <https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AEiveNr2m3k/david-rovella>

The speculative mania around Avis Budget Group erupted quickly. It collapsed 
even faster. In just two days, with no clear cause, the car-rental company’s 
stock cratered so deeply that it wiped out all but a sliver of its 600% surge 
over the previous month.

The sharp rise was, by all accounts, a classic short squeeze in which the 
hordes of investors who’d sold borrowed Avis shares, betting they’d drop, 
suddenly started racing to close out those positions by buying them back. As 
others held onto their stock, making the supply relatively scarce, the price 
soared. What caused the sudden and violent reversal—sending the stockplunging
