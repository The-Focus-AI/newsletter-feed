I was trying to figure out the last time I did one of these, and it almost exactly one year ago today, when I was in a similar chaotic headspace of dahlia planting + all the normal stuff I have to do running this newsletter and this podcast (and submitting the third draft of my book on the friendship dip, the version that finally works!)

I think of my work in seasons, and not just because of the dahlias: there are periods of intensity, and periods of loose contemplation. Right now, I'm in that period of intensity — which will bookend when I submit the book draft next week and the dahlias all go in the ground over the next two weeks (they won't flower for a solid four-six weeks, and then the intensity starts to ramp up again).

Maybe you're also in a point of intensity (hi, anyone who works in higher ed)— or a lull before the end-of-the-school year (and its various commitments) ramps up.

Hence: A GOOD OLD-FASHIONED CULTURE STUDY CHAOS THREAD.

At this point, there's a lot of built-up Culture Study chaos (for SO many reasons). I always think of these threads as descendants of the OG blog comment spaces from the 2000s/2010s (RIP The Hairpin) but you can think of them as an amalgamation of every type of Culture Study Thread. Do you want to talk about what you’re reading or cooking? GREAT. Do you want to vent or ask for advice? Also great.

You can talk about recent CS topics (your hobby, your garden), analyze recent celebrity gossip, or just post about your excitement for a burgeoning relationship or friendship. You can vent about what's hard about parenting in this moment. You can complain about how stupid it is to find a bathmat that does what you want it to and isn't grey (hi, that's me).

You can talk about the fashion choices at the Devil Wears Prada 2 premiere, or ask how to clean a very particular thing in your house, or how funny Aubrey Plaza has been on this recent press tour. You can yearn for True Spring. You can shit on Kash Patel (yes, very much allowed). You can share a cocktail recipe you've loved or a nail color that's really doing it for you. You can just do a comments version of a feelings post. Follow whatever chaos moves you.

General Culture Study thread rules abide: don’t be butts, of course, and when it comes to talking about volatile and sensitive (but important) shit, don’t mistake digital proximity for a target. Read others’ comments in good faith; ask questions about intent before assuming it, etc. etc.

We’ve somehow managed to talk about intensive parenting pracrtices here without it devolving which gives me continued hope that I can trust this very open conversation not to devolve as well. I’ll be monitoring the thread closely, and thank you all, as always, for keeping this one of the good places on the internet.
