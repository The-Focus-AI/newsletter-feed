Every once in a while, I find it helpful to pull back and reset expectations - for me and for you.

Networking, as most people think about it, doesn’t work. It feels awkward, transactional, and forced, turning relationships into a numbers game that most people can tell is off. At the same time, relationships are still the thing. Every meaningful opportunity in your career or business comes from a person, not an algorithm or a cold outreach sequence, but a real connection with someone who knows you and trusts you.

That’s the tension most people are stuck in. They know relationships matter, but they don’t want to network. This publication exists to close that gap. I write about how relationships actually work in real life, not through scripts, hacks, or surface-level advice, but through the long-term reality of staying connected to people in a way that feels natural and sustainable.

Most people don’t lose relationships because they don’t care. They lose them because they don’t have a system. They rely on memory and good intentions. They tell themselves they will reach out soon, and then life gets busy and those relationships slowly fade. Most importantly, they get in their own way, because of the stories in their head. Sound familiar?

Your relationships deserve better than that. They deserve more than a spreadsheet, more than a CRM built for sales teams, and more than a vague reminder to stay in touch. They deserve a system that reflects how relationships actually work over time.

Each week, I share clear thinking, practical tactics, and simple systems you can actually use. The goal is not to give you more to do. The goal is to help you stay connected to the right people consistently, without it feeling like work. When relationships are handled well, they compound. They open doors, create opportunities, and make your work easier and more meaningful.
