# Why you might be exhausted by AI (it's not the AI)

**From:** "Rick Mulready | The AI Playbook" <rick@rickmulready.com>
**Date:** Sun, 26 Apr 2026 14:31:16 +0000 (UTC)

Hey, it's Rick!



If you're feeling exhausted from trying to get AI to just "work"
for you and your business, you're not alone.



Some are calling it "AI fatigue," and it's real.



But after watching hundreds of people go through it first-hand,
including myself, I can tell you it's almost never about the AI.



Let's dive in...

What I'm Seeing...
------------------


Last week, one of my AI Playbook members showed up to our Q&A
call exhausted.



She’s in the middle of a big transition in her business and was
trying to map out the next six weeks with Claude's help.



To do that, she wanted a 7-day time-block plan, something she
could look at each week and know exactly what she was working on
and when.



She’d spent hours, not 20 minutes, hours, inside Claude Code
trying to build it.



She’s smart. She knows how to use AI, yet she still walked away
drained.



When I asked her what happened, she said that she “felt like she
was doing all the thinking for Claude.”



And because she’s making so many changes in her business, and
having Claude help her, she felt like she was carrying a big
cognitive load.



She was right.


Here’s what was actually going on…



She wanted Claude to take her calendar, her priorities, and her
mental and time constraints, and output a visual schedule she
could actually work from each week.



Once she was able to clearly articulate that, Claude went ahead
and did it for her



The output was exactly what she wanted.



But here was the problem…



Every time she wanted to regenerate it, she had to re-explain
everything from scratch: her schedule, her workload, her current
priorities, her constraints.



Claude had no memory of any of it.



So the tool that was supposed to save her time was actually
creating more work for her.



Claude didn’t have any background on how she works.



No context on her business model, her audience, her offers, her
voice, or her priorities.



Every session started at zero (even with Claude’s Memory
feature).



And because Claude didn’t have that foundation, it kept bouncing
ideas back to her.



Every output required a judgment call. Every response kicked a
decision back to her.

She wasn’t experiencing AI fatigue.

She was experiencing decision fatigue caused by missing
infrastructure, meaning a simple context file(s) that tells
Claude who you are, how you work, and what matters most in your
business.

The AI wasn’t the problem.

The missing context files were.

This is the most common bottleneck I see right now.



People think they’re bad at prompting, or that AI isn’t quite
good enough yet, or that they just need to try harder.



But the real issue is that they haven’t given Claude what it
needs to actually be useful.



When Claude knows your business (your offers, your audience, your
communication style, your priorities, your preferences), it stops
bouncing decisions back to you.
The cognitive load shifts off your plate because Claude can then
just do the work you want it to do.



That’s when AI stops being exhausting and starts feeling like
leverage.

Your 10-Minute Play:
--------------------


Most people go to AI with a task: “Write this. Draft that. Build
this for me.”



All well and good but the AI needs context about you if it’s
going to be helpful.



So if you haven’t already done this, before you ask Claude to do
anything, ask it what it needs from you.



Open a new chat and paste this:

I want to use you as a thinking partner and executor
for my business. Before we start, I need you to ask me the
questions that would make you most useful to me.

Ask about my business model, my audience, my offers, my
current priorities, my communication style, and anything else
you'd need to give me high-quality, personalized output — not
generic advice.

Ask me one question at a time. Once you have enough,
summarize what you know about me and how you'll apply it.

+ ◴

↑



This single conversation will help Claude understand your
specific preferences, experience and business and thus make it
way more useful to you.



Save the summary Claude gives you at the end.


* If you're using regular Claude chat, take that summary and
paste it into the "What personal preferences should Claude
consider in responses?" section in Claude Settings

From that point on, Claude will have that context in every
conversation automatically.
