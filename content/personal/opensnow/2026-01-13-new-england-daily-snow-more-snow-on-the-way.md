Light snow on Monday night dropped 1–4 inches of fresh snow
across the North Country with 1 inch reported at Loon (NH), 2
inches at Stratton (VT) and Pico (VT), 3 inches at Sunday River
(ME) and Bolton (VT), and 4 inches at Jay (VT).

The forecast calls for Tuesday to remain mostly dry with the
next round of rain and snow arriving on Tuesday night through and
Thursday. A front will first push through the region associated
with a region of low pressure over Canada on Tuesday night. This
event could drop an inch or two of snow across portions of
Vermont and New Hampshire. A larger, more widespread snowfall
event then starts on Wednesday night as another region of low
pressure tracks along the coast through southern New England into
Thursday with some 6+ inch total snows possible in the northern
Greens and Whites.

NAM model forecast animation for 7PM Tuesday through 1PM
Thursday.

Look for another forecast update on Wednesday morning. Have a
great day!

-Dr. Jay
