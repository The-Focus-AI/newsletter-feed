Significant Storm for the Southern & Eastern U.S. this Weekend
*****************************************************************

The West will largely remain in a dry pattern, while a
significant storm will impact the Southern and Eastern U.S. late
this week and into the weekend. The Southern Rockies will catch
some moderate snow initially as the storm develops, then heavy
snow will fall from the Mid-South into the Mid-Atlantic, with ski
resorts in the Southern Appalachians favored for the deepest snow
totals.

**************************************
Snow Water Equivalent (SWE), Explained
**************************************

The Snow Water Equivalent (SWE) measures the amount of water in
the snowpack and is a crucial indicator for water and resource
managers to plan for water use. SWE is the depth of water that
would cover the ground if the snow cover were in a liquid state.
We use SWE as a measure of snowpack and snow season
characteristics.
