Lake Effect Snow and Frigid Temps for the Midwest and Northeast
***************************************************************

Multiple shots of arctic air will reach the Midwest & Northeast
this week, resulting in bitterly cold temperatures (well below
zero for some areas). Lake effect snow will be favored across
Michigan, Ontario, and New York in this pattern. The West will
remain in a dry spell, though Colorado will see some light snow
this week. A strong winter storm is possible for the Mid-Atlantic
this weekend.

Japan: Big Storm Cycle On the Way
*********************************

A major storm cycle is getting underway that will continue for
at least a week, and likely more. This could be the biggest storm
cycle of the season with massive accumulations on both Honshu and
Hokkaido. A classic sea effect snow setup will deliver cold air
and copious snow totals through the end of the month.
