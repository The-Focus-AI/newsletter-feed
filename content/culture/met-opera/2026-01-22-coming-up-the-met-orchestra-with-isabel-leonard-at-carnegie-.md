**The Met Orchestra with Isabel Leonard at Carnegie Hall**

WEDNESDAY, FEBRUARY 4, 8PM

The Met Orchestra takes the storied stage of Carnegie Hall for the first time in 2026, with Music Director Yannick Nézet-Séguin on the podium to lead a program of music by 20th-century American composers.

The concert opens with Dawson's powerful Negro Folk Symphony, followed by Barber's 

**Knoxville: Summer of 1915** and "Somewhere" from 

**West Side Story**, showcasing leading mezzo-soprano Isabel Leonard. The program concludes with the spirited suite from Bernstein's jazz-inflected ballet 

**Fancy Free**.
