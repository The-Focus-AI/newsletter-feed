From The Browser 15 years ago:

The Passive In English [https://thebrowser.com/r/f7dbb2ed?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

Geoffrey Pullum | Language Log | 24 January 2011 | U

Everything there is to be known about the passive voice. Prepare to be met by the copula, the preterite, the stranded preposition. Your starter for 10: “That rash needs looking at by a specialist”. Was that a passive clause? Don’t try to rationalise any of this. As the author says, “I don’t make or try to enforce any of the rules; I am merely trying to explain what the rules seem to be” (3,613 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/a2cbe134?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])

From The Browser 9 Years Ago:

Better Wisdom From Crowds [https://thebrowser.com/r/5849ab4a?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

Peter Dizikes | MIT | 25th January 2017 | U

How to get better answers from straw polls. For a given question, you need to ask two things: What is the right answer? And, what will most other people say? For example: “Is Philadelphia the capital of Pennsylvania?” A minority will reply, correctly, “No”. But they will expect most others to reply, incorrectly, “Yes”. So when 30% say “No”, yet only 5% expect “No” to be the majority answer, then “No”, albeit a minority opinion, is the “surprisingly popular” answer — a reason to think it correct (1,500 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/424a3a1c?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])

The Biggest Ideas in the Universe | Sean Carroll [https://thebrowser.com/r/890de6e3?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

He writes out an almost unintelligible equation, Einstein’s field equation, and says, ‘I’m going to tell you everything you need to know to understand it.’ It does feel, to me, like a completely intelligible, accessible course in gravity and general relativity.”

Poem Of The Week

The Room of the Elder| Linda Hogan [https://thebrowser.com/r/f7c71ba6?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

> and the tree I planted bearing its golden nectarines this first time,
