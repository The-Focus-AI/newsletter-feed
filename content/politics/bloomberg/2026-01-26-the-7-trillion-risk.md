Japan bond crash unleashes a $7 trillion risk for global markets

In the days following last week’s Japanese bond crash, traders were still 
stunned by the speed and breadth of it all. In the Japanese government bond 
market of old, it would take weeks—sometimes months—for yields to eke out, tick 
by tick, a move of that magnitude.

But the latest selloff, which sent tremors across global markets and was 
accompanied by dramatic swings in the yen, made clearthose days are over 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-01-25/japan-bond-market-crash-raises-alarm-for-global-interest-rates>
. Traders are braced for more disorderly market swings as Japan hurtles toward a
Feb. 8 snap election 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-26/takaichi-s-ratings-dip-as-japan-heads-into-election-campaign>
—and there’s an even bigger worry for the rest of the world over the long term.
