These simple security flaws exposed Trump to another gunman.

Despite a visible security perimeter and warnings of tight security, guests said they could enter the Hilton through checkpoints on the surrounding streets by simply showing a dinner ticket or a copy of an invite to one of several predinner receptions. The tickets were reviewed by staff but weren’t scanned and there were no identification checks, attendees said. Guests were able to access the hotel lobby and lower levels without going through security scans, and only passed through magnetometers before they entered the ballroom where the dinner was held. It was  easier to get into the dinner [https://www.wsj.com/us-news/trump-shooting-correspondents-dinner-security-e1483305] than many big sports events and concert venues, report Sadie Gurman, C. Ryan Barber and Isabella Simonetti.

Big American companies are piling up profits despite war and consumer anxiety, bolstered by healthy sales growth. Q: What is Fed Chair Jerome Powell’s next move?

With Kevin Warsh’s confirmation back on track, the question is whether Powell will  give up his seat [https://www.wsj.com/economy/central-banking/fed-chair-powell-confronts-his-final-big-decision-stay-or-go-635eb131] on the Fed’s board next month. Chief economics correspondent Nick Timiraos has the latest on the Fed succession drama:

A: What’s left unresolved is whether Powell will give up his seat on the Fed’s board when his chairmanship ends or stay on as a governor, a position he can hold until January 2028.

Powell’s own test for the decision is that the criminal probe into him be “well and truly over, with transparency and finality.” Whether that threshold has been met is unclear.

If Powell leaves, Trump gets a second vacancy to fill beyond the seat being used to install Warsh. If Powell stays, Trump has no other scheduled vacancies on the Fed’s board before 2028.

Trump administration officials have said they hope and expect Powell to follow the tradition of his predecessors and leave.

People who know Powell say that he is more than eager to return to private life. But agreeing to leave soon could, at least implicitly, validate the pressure campaign Powell has spent the past year avoiding.

Staying would deny Trump another seat from which to challenge institutional norms; leaving would give Warsh room to lead the central bank and the rate-setting Federal Open Market Committee.

Powell himself hasn’t signaled which way he is leaning, and the small circle of advisers and friends who might know are declining to discuss it. Some who admire the job Powell has done think the right move now is for him to leave if the probe is truly over.

How much Regal Cinemas recently charged for opening-night seats in the best theaters to see December’s “Dune: Part Three.” Eye-popping prices for the most in-demand movies on the best screens are  becoming increasingly common [https://www.wsj.com/business/media/the-50-movie-ticket-has-arrived-42251672] as the cinema industry copies the audience-segmentation playbooks of airlines and hotels. Theaters are getting people who love movies and have discretionary income to pay substantially more. Some 17% of film tickets sold last year were for premium-format theaters with bigger screens and better sound, compared with 13% in 2021.
