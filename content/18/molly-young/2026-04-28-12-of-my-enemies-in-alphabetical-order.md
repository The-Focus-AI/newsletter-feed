# 12 of my enemies in alphabetical order

**From:** Molly Young  <mollyyoung@substack.com>
**Date:** Tue, 28 Apr 2026 19:03:20 +0000

ACCIDENTALLY OPENING THE DISHWASHER MID-CYCLE
Why does a sense of obscenity and shame wash over me when I catch my dishes “in flagrante delicto”?
THE BANALITY OF AGING
One of the many things you discover as you age is the shocking amount of people who move through life in daily physical pain.
FRIZZY BRAIN
Does your brain ever feel frizzy? Unruly, unpredictable, centripetally excited? The cure for frizzy hair (mine at least) is to drench it in water and let it reconstitute its natural pattern. I’m pretty sure the same is true of frizzy brain—that the quickest way to cure it is by immersing your whole self in water. Ideally an ocean. Sometimes a shower works.
GRAFFITI BOOKS
Not books about graffiti. Those are fine. Here I’m talking about a certain kind of (usually nonfiction) book that clearly, from page one, has no reason to exist other than as an extended graffito on the part of the author—a way for a delusionally persistent person to scrawl “I WAS HERE” on the surface of life and be comforted by a momentary reprieve in his sense of vast melancholic obscurity.
ENGLISH (LANGUAGE)
While reading a novel one day I came across a new word sough, and looked it up in the dictionary. According to the dictionary, sough means “a moaning, whistling, or rushing sound made by the wind in the trees or the sea.”
Discoveries like this offer a momentary ecstatic delusion that there is in fact a word for everything. But then, quick on its heels, comes an undoing of the delusion. Shouldn’t trees and the sea have separate words for the noises produced by their interactions with wind? Surely the physics of those sounds are different enough to merit two words I’ve never heard of instead of just one.
A second complaint about English is: the absence of a word that describes the pleasure of ripping something out by the root, like a weed or a unibrow hair.
HANGNAILS
Surely we should have evolved out of them, by now.
HYDRATING
To wake every morning in a panic of aridity and heave yourself from bed to swallow gulps of water, knowing that this will launch a daylong cycle of tiresome fluid intake followed by fluid expulsion, forever and ever without end, until bed.
INVOLUNTARY ANAGRAMMING
All day I think about anagrams. I am not bragging. This is not a trait that is comorbid with coolness or intelligence. It is not monetizable. It is a trait that (I assume) has already been rendered obsolete by AI. All it gives me is the involuntary knowledge that, for example, GAVIN NEWSOM is an anagram of SAVING WOMEN.
