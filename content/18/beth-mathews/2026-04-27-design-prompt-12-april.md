Good day! Wow, has it been a busy time around my neck of the woods. I just returned home from Crop Conference  in Austin, Texas, where I gave my first keynote talk. I was so nervous, but once I got on stage, the combination of a really kind audience and a little blacking out from the nerves made it really fun! There were several speakers throughout the day, and guess what a majority of the talks were about… personal work! I can’t emphasize the importance of doing personal work outside of our day jobs enough. It’s so good for our creative spirit, helps sharpen our design skills, and helps us discover our personal style, and if we’re lucky, can help lead to work we actually want to be doing! So I’m very grateful that you’re here and interested in these monthly design prompts. 
Another speaker at Crop was a designer named Ram Reyes . He gave a really interesting presentation on the rise of AI graphics, especially used among small businesses, and how we’re going to actually miss seeing those sad but great flyers around town that use Papyrus, Comic Sans, etc. If you ever take a look at AI-generated art, Ram mentioned that we can’t even identify what font it’s trying to emulate. Our design history is morphing into a blob of “art” with no identifiable characteristics. Ram is right. I will miss judging a sign that uses Papyrus or Comic Sans, and it’s wild how we’ll look back on those pieces of cultural paper artifacts with fondness in the coming years.
