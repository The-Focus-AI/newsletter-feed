Deep Dive: Making Money in Prediction Markets
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-28/most-prediction-market-traders-are-losing-money-while-bots-rack-up-gains>
Photographer: Graeme Sloan/Bloomberg
Prediction markets are being pitched on social media as a lucrative side hustle
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-28/most-prediction-market-traders-are-losing-money-while-bots-rack-up-gains?cmpid=042826_morningamer&utm_campaign=morningamer&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3923>
 for young Americans squeezed by rent and student debt. In reality, most 
traders are losing money, and often significant amounts.

 * The bulk of profits, among the winners, were raked in by a small group of 
what appeared to be automated bots, according to an analysis of Polymarket 
trade records, while everyone else, in aggregate, lost $131 million.
 * Polymarket has faced allegations of wash trading 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-07/polymarket-volume-inflated-by-artificial-activity-study-finds?cmpid=042826_morningamer&utm_campaign=morningamer&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3923>
, coming from customers who are seeking cryptocurrency rewards, rather than a 
return on their trading.
 * Prediction markets have quickly grown from a niche corner of finance to a 
multibillion-dollar industry attracting Wall Street capital. But a growing list 
ofinsider trading allegations 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-25/insider-trading-cases-threaten-reckoning-for-prediction-markets?cmpid=042826_morningamer&utm_campaign=morningamer&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3923>
 is bringing concerns about the platforms all the way to the White House.
 * In response, the industry is pouring money into Washington, hiring lobbyists
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-20/prediction-markets-ramp-up-washington-lobbying-to-stop-us-rules?cmpid=042826_morningamer&utm_campaign=morningamer&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3923>
 to counter criticism that their platforms are fueling a surge in gambling.
