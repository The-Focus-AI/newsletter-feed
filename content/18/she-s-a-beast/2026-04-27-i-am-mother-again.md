I’m delighted to report, as I told paid subscribers previously [https://www.shesabeast.co/r/5105de77?m=71f42b59-5c9c-43d4-809b-fcdf1c3ac9cc], that I am pregnant again! We are right on the cusp of my third trimester. Yes, thank you for asking: It’s been quite a journey so far, the first trimester was only slightly easier than last time because I was not worried that not being able to think about anything but food and TV was just my new personality now. Maybe you are wondering what all this has meant for my training, “training” being in quotes the size of the Empire State Building. Last pregnancy [https://www.shesabeast.co/r/343f1d01?m=71f42b59-5c9c-43d4-809b-fcdf1c3ac9cc], I fought to keep things as normal as possible, which was equally attributable to my insanity and need to preserve my sanity.

This time around, I’m taking it much easier. Taking care of an infant, now toddler, is very physical—we’re only just getting to the point that we don’t have to carry him bodily everywhere [https://www.shesabeast.co/r/a4a3e135?m=71f42b59-5c9c-43d4-809b-fcdf1c3ac9cc]. I was much more sedentary and stiff pre-kid, and all the getting up and down from the floor and picking up and carrying a wiggly being around (with good form) has honestly done wonders for me. Lifting right now is a lot more like “staying in practice”: I took a good two months off during my first trimester, and then picked back up one or two days a week since then. The weights are still “heavy,” but I haven’t touched more than 100 pounds in many months. It feels more important this time around for the workout to feel achievable than challenging.

For someone used to treating their body however they want, pregnancy has, let’s say, forced me to learn some difficult lessons the hard way (as is my custom [https://www.shesabeast.co/r/da121b4c?m=71f42b59-5c9c-43d4-809b-fcdf1c3ac9cc]). I have to be really deliberate about managing my energy, and it’s hard for a “why walk when you can run” person to be waddling around. I feel accompanied by the old Super Mario World pipe music [https://www.shesabeast.co/r/747f162e?m=71f42b59-5c9c-43d4-809b-fcdf1c3ac9cc] at all times. Last time I also learned a lot of lifts to avoid that cause me pains (single-leg movements, deep squatting after a certain point, Copenhagen planks).[^1]

On the plus side, thanks to lifting, I’ve never had back pain. I can handle the current baby plus the pregnant stomach pretty easily, as well as all the getting up and down and carrying. A 16-month old makes for an excellent weight to snatch into the air for sets of 10, and it makes him scream with delight. It all has made me intensely glad I came into this with so much strength that I can coast on.

Due to the impending baby, plus the delightfully frenzied “essentially having a head injury” experience of shepherding a newborn through their first few months of life, plus the overall difficult experience I had working through my last maternity leave and promoting/touring a book at the same time, I’m hoping to have better boundaries this go-round. Therefore, She’s A Beast will be on hiatus from the beginning of July until the end of December (pending a predictable landing of the baby plane, knock on wood).

As I wrote to the paid subscribers yesterday, I’m incredibly grateful to this community, and especially the subscribers, who make it possible for our family to take care of each other through these incredibly valuable, irreplaceable periods. To be honest, I never imagined I could love being a parent so much. While I don’t really talk about the practicalities publicly, in the background, it has radically reshaped and deepened and enriched so much of the thinking that goes into my work [https://www.shesabeast.co/r/2f4a7f30?m=71f42b59-5c9c-43d4-809b-fcdf1c3ac9cc] (and likewise, getting to do this work truly makes me a better parent). So thank you for that, deeply and sincerely.
