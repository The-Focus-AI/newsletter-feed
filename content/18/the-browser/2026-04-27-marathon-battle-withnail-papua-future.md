The Art Of Letting Go [https://thebrowser.com/r/4395a268?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

Rosie Sultan | Pangyrus | 27th April 2026 | U

Living with cancer and a life expectancy of "five to ten years" is fraught. Who to tell, and when, and how? Letting go can be harder than holding on. "Marathon runners know this. They let go of their doubt at mile twenty, their exhaustion at mile twenty-three, their need to look graceful as they cross the finish line. They hang on to their next step, and the one after that — but they let go of everything else" (1,700 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/41ebcbc5?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])

The Importance Of The Battle Of Savo Island [https://thebrowser.com/r/7ace88c6?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

John Severini & Stephen Biddle | War On The Rocks | 27th April 2026 | U

Other than Pearl Harbor, the Battle of Savo Island in 1942 is the worst defeat in US naval history. The American fleet was superior in every way — more ships, with more advanced weapons and technology — but was rapidly outgunned by Japan because of failures in command and operation. It's a valuable lesson: "Acquiring a weapon or a sensor is not the same as knowing how to use it" (3,000 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/13a3711b?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])

----------------------------------------

 

Withnail's Coat & I [https://thebrowser.com/r/49af9a60?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

Adam Scovell | On The Row | 24th April 2026 | U

The full-skirted, increasingly battered tweed coat that Richard E. Grant's character wears in Withnail and I is "one of the most famous items of men’s clothing to grace British screens". It always looks fantastic, even when Withnail himself is at his lowest ebb, railing against the world through an alcoholic stupor. The coat had a strange afterlife: auctioned for charity, worn by a DJ, endlessly recreated (3,600 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/ef506035?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])

----------------------------------------

 

Bridging West Papua [https://thebrowser.com/r/e260805a?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

Siti Amrina Rosada & Robbani Amal Romis | Failed Architecture | 25th March 2026 | U

The Youtefa Bridge connects the extremities of Youtefa Bay in West Papua, Indonesia. It vastly reduces the travel time through the urban sprawl of nearby Jayapura. It has also been disruptive and harmful: affecting the mangrove forests and forcing land sales by surrounding indigenous peoples. Refusing "interconnectedness" is a way for West Papuans to resist Indonesian dominance (2,600 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/2553f3e7?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])

from The Browser nine years ago:

Ten Year Futures [https://thebrowser.com/r/b910704f?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

Benedict Evans | 25th April 2017 | U

Futurology. Yes, we are moving towards a world of autonomous vehicles, virtual reality and artificial intelligence — but that’s ten years away. In the shorter term expect further disruption of vulnerable industries from existing technologies. Television has so far has “not really been touched by the internet”, but is “starting to look unstable”. And online retailing is undermining “broad classes” of traditional retail (1,900 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/eeeeb4d8?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])
