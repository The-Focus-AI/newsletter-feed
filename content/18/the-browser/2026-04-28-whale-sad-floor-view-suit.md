How To Euthanise A Whale [https://thebrowser.com/r/15767226?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

Freda Kreier | Undark | 27th April 2026 | U

Beached whales go into shock and suffocate under their own weight, taking days to die. Killing them is an act of mercy, but can be complicated. In Australia, explosives are placed near their brains. New Zealand uses a repurposed WWII-era anti-tank weapon to pierce their skulls. In the US, the best practice now is to inject potassium chloride straight into a whale’s heart after a cocktail of sedatives (2,400 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/9064d8d0?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])

If America’s So Rich, How’d It Get So Sad? [https://thebrowser.com/r/a42137a9?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

Derek Thompson | 23rd April 2026 | U

“The United States was a reasonably happy country for a long time. It is not happy now.” The pandemic has left a “permademic” in its wake. These “tragic twenties” are characterised by years of above-average inflation, a news tone that is “more surprisingly negative than at any period of news on record” and the “breaking apart of social trust”. The last one has historically been an effect of pandemics (3,900 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/f8717634?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])

----------------------------------------

 

My Year On A Factory Floor Assembly Line [https://thebrowser.com/r/65c5ff47?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

Aella | Knowingless | 16th April 2026 | U

Sobering look at a year spent assembling electrical relays on a factory floor. “You couldn’t zone out and just do autopilot, because you had to follow a set of instructions with slight variations that change a little each time. You had to pay attention. But the attention you paid was not interesting; it was ongoing, it was relentless, it was stupid. My mind wasn’t mine. I made more mistakes than almost anyone else” (3,300 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/651820e9?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])

----------------------------------------

 

Ignorant Art Historian: View Of Notre Dame [https://thebrowser.com/r/d24055e8?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

Hal Foster | Paris Review | 28th April 2026 | U

Art critic demystifies the act of seeing an artwork. “As we approach this painting, we have little idea of what it depicts, or whether it depicts anything at all. The title offers a kind of lifeline: View of Notre Dame. But what kind of view and from where? Neither abstract nor representational, the painting requires a shift in our way of looking: its elements are less images of things than signs for them” (800 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/66ae0d3b?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])

Saddam’s Tailor Sews On [https://thebrowser.com/r/c1097203?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd]

Joseph Hammond | Roads & Kingdoms | 25th April 2014 | U

Smart potentates buy their suits from Recep Cesur of Istanbul, who learned tailoring in his home town of Diyarbakir, Turkey, opened a shop in Baghdad, and snagged a prize client: Saddam Hussein. Saddam bought 80 suits, including the one worn at his trial. “His shoes were 11.6, his trousers 54, his jacket 56.” Cesur has also sold suits to Pervez Musharraf, Hamid Karzai and Nelson Mandela (1,100 words [https://thebrowser.com/r/2fa7da73?m=870013e5-9af6-40f5-bf0e-51ce412162bd])
