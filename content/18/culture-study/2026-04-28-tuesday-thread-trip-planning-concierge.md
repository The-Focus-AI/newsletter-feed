OFFER: Logistical help on hut-to-hut hiking in the Dolomites

Last year I was interviewing someone for my book about putting together elaborate week-long friend trips, and how much planning goes into them. I asked him if it ever became annoying, or cumbersome, and he said: I love doing logistics.

In that second, I realized: I, too, love logistics. LOVE THEM. I'm the person who offers to search for plane tickets for someone else. And I love them even more now that I'm not doing them all the time for work travel. And I know there are many others like us in this audience: people who love helpings others figure out order of operations, whether they should rent a car or take public transport, opt for an Airbnb or hotel, how many days they should spend in a place, where the best playground is to burn off some energy, whether you should fly into LGA (yes), JFK (maybe), or Newark (no).

I also know that there are many people in this audience who are very good at so many other things....but find these sorts of logistics absolutely overwhelming, even paralyzing. So allow us to help one another! Merge our skills! It could be for a trip you're taking next week or one you want to theoretically want to take for your 50th birthday (five years from now). It could be a day trip or a month-long meander. We're here to help!

Let's try this formatting style:

If you want advice, you can phrase it this way —

ASK: Looking for help planning a trip to Glacier National Park with one active grandparent and one with limited walking ability....and two kids under 5. The hope is to fly in [MONTH] and stay for [HOW LONG]. If we want to camp, when should we reserve spots? Should we stay in the park? Any other tips?

[In short, be specific about what you're looking for, who's going, any other stipulations — the more specific you are, the better the advice will be]

And if you're offering advice —

OFFER: Expertise about mid-tier hotels in Barcelona, preferred flight itineraries when flying from the West Coast to Europe, Greek ferry info, hiking the Haute Route and the Alta Via 1, Cambodia and Laos tips, strong feelings about which Caye to visit in Belize, day and weekend trips all over Washington State, driving in Western Norway, roadtripping in Montana, and how to go to a concert at The Gorge As An Adult Who Enjoys Sleeping....

You get the picture. ...
