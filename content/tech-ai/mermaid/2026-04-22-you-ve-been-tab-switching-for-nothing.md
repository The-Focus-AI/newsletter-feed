# 🤦 You've been tab-switching for nothing

**From:** Mermaid <hello@mermaid.ai>
**Date:** Wed, 22 Apr 2026 14:32:15 +0000

---

Hey Mermaiders 👋 *W**here does your diagram actually live after you make it?*

Most people build in Mermaid, then export or copy-paste their way into wherever it needs to go. That works. But it also means your diagram and your doc are immediately out of sync the moment anything changes.

We have a **full plugin ecosystem** – VS Code, Confluence, Jira, Google Docs, and more – and it's designed to keep Mermaid as the source of truth while extending that power into the tools your team already lives in. Build once, show up everywhere. This month is a full map of that.
**🔌 ****PLUGINS 101** 
**The Mermaid ecosystem, explained.
=================================**

Mermaid's plugin layer runs in *two directions*:

- **Pull in. **You're already in VS Code, or mid-ticket in Jira. You can create and edit Mermaid diagrams right there – full editor, AI generation, the works – without opening a new tab. Your diagram syncs back to Mermaid automatically. 
- **Push out. **You built something in Mermaid and need it in a Confluence doc or a Google Doc. Insert it directly. When the diagram updates in Mermaid, it updates in the page. No re-exporting and no screenshot-as-attachment that goes stale in two sprints. 

**The works: **VS Code, Claude Code, Cursor, ChatGPT, Windsurf, JetBrains, Confluence, Jira, Google Docs, Microsoft Word, and PowerPoint. Get the plugin party started.

**💻 VS CODE** 
**Your IDE called – it wants to make diagrams.
============================================**

The VS Code plugin is probably the one your engineering team is most likely to actually use because it doesn't ask them to leave where they already are. You can describe a diagram in plain language and have it rendered in seconds – but the more interesting part is what it can do with your actual code.

Mention @mermaid-chart in GitHub Copilot Chat and point it at a file, a folder, or a schema. It'll generate an ER diagram from your database structure, a cloud architecture diagram from your config files, a Docker diagram from your Compose setup, or a C4 diagram from your entire codebase – automatically. When the code changes, it detects the diff and offers to update the diagram with one click.

The diagram stays in Mermaid. The work stays in VS Code.

[ Get the VS Code extension](https://f1gxg.r.a.d.sendibm1.com/mk/cl/f/sh/7nVU1aA2nfuMScVBZPeaqywbTgIZOga/XINS2JE-XEHk)
**🔥 CLAUDE** 
**The fastest diagram you'll ever make.
=====================================**

Mermaid has an official connector in Claude – which means you can describe a flow, a system, an architecture, and get a rendered diagram back without leaving the conversation. The connector validates your Mermaid syntax, renders it as a PNG, and generates a shareable playground link so collaborators can open and edit it directly in Mermaid. It works across Claude, Claude Desktop, Claude Code, and the Claude API.

For teams already working in Claude: diagrams are now just part of the conversation, not a separate step.

[ Add the Mermaid connector](https://f1gxg.r.a.d.sendibm1.com/mk/cl/f/sh/7nVU1aA2nfwFS90UXyt2tLGPe2nM8d6/rDSoP3-35MYA)
****💡** CONFLUENCE + JIRA** 
**One source of truth. Zero re-exporting.
=======================================**

When you insert a Mermaid diagram into a Confluence page or Jira ticket, it's a live link to the source – not a screenshot. Edit it in Mermaid and the page reflects it. Edit it from inside Confluence or Jira and Mermaid reflects that too. One source of truth, no re-exporting, no hunting down every place a screenshot got pasted.

That means architecture docs, onboarding wikis, and runbooks that actually keep up with the product. Or it means a diagram panel inside any ticket – pull from your existing library or build inline, without leaving the ticket.
