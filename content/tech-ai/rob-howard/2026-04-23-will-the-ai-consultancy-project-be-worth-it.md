--------------------------------------------------
"How Much Should My Clients (or I) Invest in L&D?"
--------------------------------------------------

Set realistic expectations for potential clients (& yourself)
with these L&D Benchmarks

No one advances their career or business without improving
themselves. Formally, we call that professional development. And,
if that's done for an organization – then we call it learning and
development (L&D).

Experienced folks already know this – however, if you or your
clients don't have an established habit or budget of doing this,
then it's natural to focus a bit on cost.

Now, being cognizant of cost isn't inherently a bad thing.
However, it can turn into unintentional self-sabotage…if your
clients (or you) do it without objective benchmarks and data.

If you've seen the AI Consultancy program page (
https://innovatingwithai.com/ai-consultancy/?ck_subscriber_id=3998817959
), then you know we love data 📊😊.

However, I didn't get to share benchmarks on how much your
potential clients (and yourself) should invest in ongoing
learning & development.

Here's the math:

--------------------------------
Example A – 1-5% of total salary
--------------------------------

(Source: Success Coaching, How To Build a Budget for an L&D
Initiative)

The Benchmark: In general, HR experts would tell your potential
clients to figure out the total amount spent on salaries, then
spend 1-5% of that on learning and development.

Your Potential Clients' Learning Budget: If they spend $1M/year
on salaries, they should invest $10K-$50K/year on learning and
development (which might include all or some of your AI
consulting services).

Your Learning Budget: Either you already are or will be the CEO
of your consultancy. Using this standard, your learning budget
would be 1-5% of your (solo) consultancy revenue goal.

That said, if your goal is $250,000, then your 1-5% learning
budget = $2,500 – $12,500 per year.

Here's another scenario though…

--------------------------------
Example B – 3–6% of total salary
--------------------------------

(Source: OneRange, Corporate learning vs upskilling: How much to
invest)

Learning & development budgets change based on business
objectives. If the goal is training to maintain standards
(necessary for very regulated industries and franchises), then
you spend less.

If the goal is upskilling and innovation (i.e. artificial
intelligence), then they recommend you spend more on it. Which,
makes sense, because the ROI would be higher relative to
compliance training.

The Benchmark: "a minimum of 3%...and up to 6% if you can afford
it" with a minimum of $2,500 per employee.

Your Potential Clients' Learning Budget: If they're spending $1M
on salaries, then they should have a $30,000 – $60,000 learning
budget they can invest in your AI consulting services.

Your Learning Budget: If your solo consultancy goal is $250,000,
then 3-6% learning budget would be $7,500 – $15,000 (per year).

Now, benchmarks aside, it probably won't inspire your clients if
all you tell them is "you should spend this much".
