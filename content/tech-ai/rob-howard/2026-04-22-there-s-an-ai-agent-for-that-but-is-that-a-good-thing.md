--------------------------
AI Agents – What Are They?
--------------------------

TL;DR:

AI Tools: software that leverages AI to do specific tasks (with
significant human input)

AI Agents: Autonomous entities that use AI to interact with and
perceive environments, make decisions, and achieve particular
goals (with minimal human input)

Details:

Like I’ve said before, AI tools are like your car's cruise
control while AI agents are like self-driving cars.

Cruise control helps with a specific task – maintaining a
constant speed without you having to keep your foot on the
accelerator. It's an application of technology (like an AI tool)
that makes driving easier and more efficient for a particular
function. However, it still requires the driver to be in control
and make decisions about steering, braking, and navigating.

A self-driving car, on the other hand, uses a combo of sensors,
machine learning, and decision-making algorithms to navigate,
recognize traffic signals, avoid obstacles, and reach a
destination with minimal human input. Effectively, it's an
autonomous system that perceives its environment, makes
decisions, and takes actions to achieve the goal of safely
transporting passengers.

Now, I use this car analogy, because it's literally a physical
example in an environment where it's easier to observe the
mechanical labor being performed.

When it comes to how AI agents will affect professionals,
businesses, and AI consultants, well – many of those use cases
will be in a digital environment where visualizing the mental
labor of knowledge work isn't quite as intuitive.

So to make the abstract way more concrete, we need…

--------------------------------------
A Practical Example: Overdue Invoices…
--------------------------------------

Ok, say you or a business needs to handle overdue invoices, there
are 3 ways you could do it:

* Manual (Past)
* AI Tools (Present)
* AI Agent (Future)

Let's take a slightly closer look:

1 – Manual (Past)

* Log into accounting software
* Search for overdue accounts
* Draft reminder emails
* Send them one by one
* Track responses

2 – AI Tools (Present)

* Still needs human direction at every step
* Can help draft emails faster
* But requires manual coordination

3 – AI Agent (Future)

* Automatically checks for overdue invoices
* Drafts personalized follow-ups
* Sends reminders on schedule
* Updates payment status
* Alerts you only when needed

The agent doesn't just help with tasks – it manages entire
workflows autonomously:

You don't check in with it – it checks in with you.

That's the ideal scenario.

However, like it is with any technology, it won't be perfect when
you start using it. There's always a transition period, which, if
you have any experience in rolling out updates to systems,
technological or otherwise, you already know.

That said, this is usually where a lot can go wrong for clients,
because, in a digital environment, they have less familiarity,
which makes visualizing it much harder. And the less they can see
it, is the less they can see potential risks, actual threats, and
opportunities in that environment.

How To Find Hidden Opportunities
--------------------------------

There's a hidden relationship between the "past", "present", and
"future" ways of doing things. If you think critically about it,
both AI Tools and AI Agents build on the "past" that came before
it.

That might sound like a "Captain Obvious" statement, but stay
with me.

AI, in many ways, is a force multiplier of an underlying process,
which means scaling the throughput of whatever workflow you apply
it to will also scale any inefficiencies or errors within that
process.

So, sometimes you'll need to temporarily slow clients down, by
mapping their existing workflow, before deciding where, when, and
how to apply AI tools and AI agents to it.

When you have a clear picture of not just the workflow, but the
stakeholders involved, as well as externalities and invisible
dependencies your client hasn't thought of in years because the
'guy that managed that' got let go a year or two ago, is when
you'll have a clearer picture of the business environment you're
working within.

This can reveal hidden opportunities for AI improvements that can
turn into an extra 3–12 months of paid engagements.

Will you need this level of detail all the time? Nah. But it also
depends on the industry you're in, as well as company size, stage
