Story Time: Me, 14 years ago in Washington, DC...
-------------------------------------------------

The Rob you’re looking at there was making $50K per year as a
solo developer and lovestruck for the woman sitting next to him.
They didn’t have much - just a 1-bedroom apartment between the
two of them and a daily routine.

She’d go off to work at a DC accounting firm.
He’d work remotely from the kitchen countertop.

It wasn’t much, and yet, I felt like I had it all. I knew she was
the one for me, and I was determined to create a life we’d always
be grateful for.

As for what that looked like…well, if anything, I had a much
better idea of what I didn’t want:

❌ 60-80+ hour work weeks that kept us apart.

❌ Saying no to vacations because I had to "put in extra weekend
work."

❌ To become an “absent” dad and partner who, even when in the
room, was “away in his head at work.”

❌ Having zero time or energy to give back to the causes we cared
about.

❌ Scraping by to make ends meet, constantly stressed about what
came next.

I was willing to do whatever it took to not end up like this.

I wanted more.

And after over a decade of ‘figuring it out’, I can say this…

-------------------------------------------------------------
Now: I have more time to date my wife (and hang with our son)
-------------------------------------------------------------

Fast forward to today and life looks very different.

My smile is even wider than the first pic because hey, the love
of my life became my wife, and we have a son who knows he’s loved
by both his parents.

That dream life we envisioned in our first apartment?

After years of choosing ‘the path less traveled’, it became a
reality: I built a $2M/year service freedom business.

One that allows me to do things like:

* Date my wife all over again at our old date spots
* Take father-son trips to hangout with Pokémon in Japan
* "Bucket-list family trips" to places like Antarctica

I’m talking about the kind of freedom where I take my wife to
revisit all our old date spots, while taking my son to tour the
Smithsonian museums, and capping it all off with a family beach
trip to Delaware.

Great times… and whenever I got back to my home office, there is
NO mountain pile-up of work – my systems and team take care of
it.

Email?

Done in less than 1 hour.

Instead of feeling chained to the chair on a Monday, I'm thankful
for everything that got done while I was away.

Life is very different now.

Back then, I made $50K a year and was $20,000 in credit-card
debt.

Then, just 1 year later, I made $175K – that 3.5X jump happened
because I decided to stop ‘fucking around with freelancing’ and
finally get serious.

(btw, all the client and consultancy skills + systems I built
back then are all part of the Trusted Advisor Skills Engine™ - 14
years later, they’re still relevant, even with AI).

It's interesting… because I’ve had friends who were paid just as
well, even more so, but they rarely had all 4 freedoms...

* Freedom of Time: Spend your days on things that matter—whether
that’s work, family, or exploring something new.
* Freedom of Money: Build stability for yourself and your loved
ones while enjoying the life you’ve worked for.
* Freedom of Relationships: Surround yourself with people who
uplift you and align with your values.
* Freedom of Purpose: Live a life that’s meaningful to you,
whether through your work, loved ones, or giving back.

Now, after having done it, and repeatedly showing others to do
it, I can confidently say I’ve found a timeless way to do it.

Investing in yourself.
Finding the right mentor.
Committing to your goals.

To create lasting change in life, these things have always been
true. Do it right, and a life of flexible freedom isn't some
pie-in-the-sky dream. It's doable so long as you're willing to
put in the work.
