-----------------------------------------------------------------



I see this pattern a lot in my community.



Someone's got a course with great completion rates, awesome
testimonials, and students that get great results.



But sales are flat, and they're starting to wonder if they should
create a whole new offer.



So what do they do?



They start adding bonus content, creating more resources, stuff
like that.



But if the data showed you that 10,000 people visited your sales
page last month and only 14 bought...



Your problem isn't the course.



It's getting more qualified traffic to the page. Or it's your
positioning. Or your pricing strategy.



The course itself is working fine.



But you won't know that unless you actually look at what the data
is telling you.



And here's the thing, you probably KNOW your sales are flat.



You've been staring at that number for months.



But knowing you have a problem isn't the same as knowing WHICH
problem to solve.



That's where most business owners spin their wheels.



Here's the AI strategy most people are missing:



You canâ€™t have AI to do the work in your business until you know
what work needs to be done.


This is where AI becomes your business analyst instead of just
your task executor.



Towards the end of the podcast interview, I suggested the hosts
have their students (who are non-techy, btw) set up a simple GPT
in ChatGPT or Claude Project or Gemini Gem (same concept).



Give it instructions similar to what I shared above and then they
can feed their monthly stats to the GPT.



Every month, they screenshot their rental dashboard, drop it into
the GPT, and it gives them:



1. What's working that they should keep doing

2. What's the biggest bottleneck right now

3. The one thing to fix this month that moves the needle



Takes them three minutes.



No overwhelm.



No guessing.



Just clarity on what actually matters.



You're not asking AI to fix everything. You're asking it to find
your biggest bottleneck.



Your business has one bottleneck. AI can find it in 30 seconds.
Then you stop guessing and start growing.
-----------------------------------------------------------------



And here's where it gets interesting for you.



Many of the nearly 600 members in my Community came in thinking
they needed AI to execute things faster. Write emails quicker.
Repurpose content in half the time. Generate more ideas.



And of course, AI does all of that (really well).



But the strategic shift happens when you realize AI can also tell
you what's worth executing in the first place.



Because speed without direction is just doing the wrong things
faster.



You probably have data sitting in your business right now that
would tell you exactly where your bottleneck is.



You might be looking at the data every month. Maybe you're even
tracking it in a dashboard somewhere.



But are you asking it the right question?



Not "How do I get better at everything?"



But something like:



"Based on what's actually happening in my business right now,
what's the one thing I should focus on improving that will move
the needle most?"



Let AI help you interpret your stats and data, then use it to
tackle what needs the most attention in your business.



What data are you tracking that you wish you understood better?
