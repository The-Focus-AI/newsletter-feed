# I almost threw my laptop across the room (here's why)

**From:** "Rick Mulready | The AI Playbook" <rick@rickmulready.com>
**Date:** Sun, 01 Feb 2026 15:04:13 +0000 (UTC)

---

Hey, it's Rick!



Last Friday morning, I sat down to write this newsletter using
the workflow I'd built the previous week in Claude Code.



I was on my second coffee (or third?) and was pumped to dive in.



The week before, I'd spent hours building out my newsletter
system.



It pulled themes from my Q&A transcripts and notes from
throughout the week, gave me a solid outline.



The outline it gave me that first week was awesome. The workflow
took just minutes to run once it was built.



So Friday rolls around, I fire up the same workflow, and...



The output was terrible.



It was like Claude and I'd never talked before.



â˜•â˜•â˜•



I spent the next couple hours trying to fix it.



Revising instructions.



Trying different prompts.



Going in circles.



Same workflow I'd built and worked so well the previous week.
Same files. Same AI. Completely different results.



I honestly wanted to throw my laptop across the room.



Finally, I just closed it and walked away.





And then it hit me.



AI isn't inconsistent. I was. My system was broken.



The AI was doing exactly what I asked.



But I'd missed something in the system/workflow I created.



Your AI isn't broken. Your system you created to accomplish your
task is.
-----------------------------------------------------------------





So I audited the system. Looked at every step, read through all
the instructions, checked the context files and examples.



Here's what I found:



My instructions weren't clear enough about which examples to
model. The specific newsletter structure I wanted wasn't
well-defined.



The AI was being consistent. My system wasn't.



So I decided to have Claude interview me. What did I actually
want this newsletter to sound like? What structure? What tone?
What context did it need access to?



Then we cross-referenced those answers against my existing
instructions. Updated every mismatch. One step at a time.



Now it works. Every Friday, same workflow, consistently good
output.



No more laptop-throwing urges.



No more circling.



Just results.



It's not the AI. It's the system you created.

When your outputs are inconsistent, something in your setup
is broken:

â€¢ Instructions aren't specific enough

â€¢ Context is missing or wrong

â€¢ Steps are out of order

â€¢ Examples don't match expectations

â€¢ Constraints aren't defined

Real example from my workflow:

from my Community (their "Top 3 Things" I call it).



I also have a content memory directory with all my past
newsletters.



I give Claude Cowork access to both.



Then I run a simple prompt:



Analyze member feedback patterns in my 3 Things Google
Sheet.

Generate the following:

â€¢ 5 email ideas

â€¢ 5 YouTube video ideas

It works because it has memory (prevents repetition), clear
inputs (the Sheet + directory), and defined constraints (no
duplicates).



Same principle works for you too:



Give AI access to your inputs (customer feedback, past work,
preferences) + memory of what you've already created +
constraints on what you don't want repeated.














And of course, you're never simply copy/pasting the output AI
writes for you and publishing.



Once AI does it's work, then you get to personalize and customize
it to you.



I see this pattern constantly in my community.
----------------------------------------------



Someone builds an AI workflow that works once, then mysteriously
stops working.



And they blame the AI.



If your results vary, something's missing from the workflow you
created, or was incomplete from the start.





The question isn't "Why isn't AI working?" It's "What's missing
in the system I created?"


When your AI workflow isn't working, audit these six things:



AI Workflow Diagnostic Checklist:

â€¢ Instructions: Are they specific enough? Do they include
examples of "good"?

â€¢ Context: What files or data are you providing? Is
anything missing or outdated?

â€¢ Steps: Are they in the right order? Any unnecessary
steps?

â€¢ Examples: Have you shown the AI what success looks
like?

â€¢ Constraints: Have you defined what you DON'T want?

â€¢ Memory: Does the AI have access to previous context it
needs?

This checklist will help you discover exactly what to audit.
