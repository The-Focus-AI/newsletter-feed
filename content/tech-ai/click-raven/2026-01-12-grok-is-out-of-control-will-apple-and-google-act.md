**What‚Äôs happening**
Google says AI Overviews don‚Äôt appear by default in Search.

According to Robby Stein, Google actively reduces AI Overviews for query types where users don‚Äôt click, engage, or find them useful.

If people consistently ignore an Overview, the system learns and stops showing it for those searches.

**Why it matters**
This explains why AI Overview visibility can fluctuate without any clear algorithm update. 

The presence (or absence) of Overviews often reflects user behavior, not ranking changes. It also clarifies why some informational queries still trigger Overviews while navigational or image-heavy searches (like athlete names) often don‚Äôt.

**How the system works**
Google sometimes runs **‚Äúunder the hood‚Äù expanded queries** beyond what users type, pulling in related context and sub-answers. 

That‚Äôs why pages can appear in AI citations even without matching exact keywords. 

Personalization exists, but Google says it‚Äôs intentionally limited to keep results consistent across users.
