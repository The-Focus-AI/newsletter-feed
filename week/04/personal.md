## Major Topics and Stories

### 1. Arctic Blast and Extreme Cold Warning for Northeast
**Coverage:** [OpenSnow (New England)](https://opensnow.com/dailysnow/newengland/post/42422?utm_source=internal&utm_medium=email&utm_term=dailysnowalert&utm_campaign=2025-2026), [OpenSnow (Regional)](https://lift.opensnow.com/newsletters/east/2026-01-19/index.html)

The New England Daily Snow forecast warned of an unprecedented cold snap approaching the region, with meteorologist Dr. Jay Cordeira expressing rare concern about the severity of the forecasted conditions.

> "The latest forecast for the mountains (e.g., Killington and Jay Peak below) is for windchill temperatures between –55F and –25F on Friday (24 Jan) through Monday (26 Jan). If this forecast verifies, we'll likely see a couple resorts take actions around potential cold exposure. It's not fun skiing with air temperatures of –20F to –10F and windchills below –30F; it's quite the opposite." — [Dr. Jay Cordeira, OpenSnow](https://opensnow.com/dailysnow/newengland/post/42422?utm_source=internal&utm_medium=email&utm_term=dailysnowalert&utm_campaign=2025-2026)

The forecast also noted a relatively quiet week for snowfall in the New England region, with only modest accumulations of 1-3 inches expected across the Green and White Mountains from upslope flow patterns on Monday night and again Wednesday night through Thursday morning.

### 2. Lake Effect Snow Patterns Across Midwest and Great Lakes
**Coverage:** [OpenSnow (Regional)](https://lift.opensnow.com/newsletters/east/2026-01-19/index.html)

The broader regional newsletter highlighted how multiple shots of arctic air would create prime conditions for lake effect snow across Michigan, Ontario, and New York, while most of the western United States remained in a dry spell.

> "Multiple shots of arctic air will reach the Midwest & Northeast this week, resulting in bitterly cold temperatures (well below zero for some areas). Lake effect snow will be favored across Michigan, Ontario, and New York in this pattern." — [OpenSnow](https://lift.opensnow.com/newsletters/east/2026-01-19/index.html)

### 3. Major Storm Cycle Developing in Japan
**Coverage:** [OpenSnow (Regional)](https://lift.opensnow.com/newsletters/east/2026-01-19/index.html)

While North America faced arctic cold, Japan was experiencing the opposite extreme—a potentially historic snowfall event.

> "A major storm cycle is getting underway that will continue for at least a week, and likely more. This could be the biggest storm cycle of the season with massive accumulations on both Honshu and Hokkaido. A classic sea effect snow setup will deliver cold air and copious snow totals through the end of the month." — [OpenSnow](https://lift.opensnow.com/newsletters/east/2026-01-19/index.html)

### 4. Mid-Atlantic Storm Tracking for Sunday January 25
**Coverage:** [OpenSnow (New England)](https://opensnow.com/dailysnow/newengland/post/42422?utm_source=internal&utm_medium=email&utm_term=dailysnowalert&utm_campaign=2025-2026)

The extended forecast identified a potential significant winter storm for the Mid-Atlantic region on Sunday, January 25, though model uncertainty remained about whether it would track north enough to impact New England. The analysis noted that both ECMWF AI ensemble and GFS ensemble models were beginning to converge on a solution keeping the storm south of New England, but emphasized the need to monitor trends.
