# Week 04 Politics Newsletter Analysis (January 19-25, 2026)

## Overview

Week 04 was dominated by two major crises: the escalating Minneapolis ICE operations culminating in two fatal shootings of US citizens, and Trump's aggressive Greenland acquisition campaign that rattled markets before a vague "framework" deal was announced. [Semafor DC](https://semafor.com/s/vDzVxB0xJJ?email=wschenk@gmail.com) reported Senate Democrats threatening to block DHS funding, while [Bloomberg Weekend](https://links.message.bloomberg.com/s/c/BuO-1MubV5TliQy1ZQnDAzK_WHGF8yLF-COPG66KQgwAbImGusZhYLqQGzTDyUTZlP6dS_bm7woB4_Vwon2HHqEEkggQmA_jAAxrTcRiCHGrOyaTyWfgx1fFlFZ1vPXsSZ4f3pNfpL1mkZjfEnh72HP2cVpo98_QY3R4fwn4feCxcEo4iJpHCGfSXt6qhOtu_GDplLymd3r6jFQN0RzD1aXLQOd3atiGGoPUV5Zbp1B8hFZ5zeT-Bow2K211gcPAUo3i1wQx9Ur4ZumfBJU64CxV59mkdg_O7X8WMQzXNJ5L5XWKikSe-Qpgoz2VZSMuUnVaX1hNyjDyAM8HNtPLkKHAQIkXEcN0WiUBXfvPg0jTEPOfqc3RcPML2g/4kKDqnAdWamMyBdanr25k5oTZ89xDfnJ/17) tracked market whiplash as Trump dropped tariff threats against Europe. ## Major Topics and Stories

### 1. Minneapolis ICE Crisis: Two Citizens Killed

**Coverage:** [Semafor DC](https://semafor.com/s/vDzVxB0xJJ?email=wschenk@gmail.com), [Compact](https://www.compactmag.com/r/1160b5b2?m=33b1996b-f427-4fb3-88a9-83a2095d7637), The Atlantic Daily, Emma Tucker/WSJ, [Semafor DC - Crunch Time](https://semafor.com/s/zrsmp2aIeN?email=wschenk@gmail.com)

The week saw Minneapolis become ground zero for the most intense confrontation between federal immigration enforcement and local resistance since Trump's second term began. Two US citizens were fatally shot by federal agents: Renee Good (shot earlier in January) and Alex Pretti (shot January 25). The escalation triggered a potential government shutdown as Senate Democrats threatened to block DHS funding.

> "Senate Democrats will not provide the votes to proceed to the appropriations bill if the DHS funding bill is included." — [Semafor DC](https://semafor.com/s/vDzVxB0xJJ?email=wschenk@gmail.com)

[Compact](https://www.compactmag.com/r/1160b5b2?m=33b1996b-f427-4fb3-88a9-83a2095d7637) offered a notably contrarian perspective from Alicia Nieves, a former rapid-response network organizer, who argued that immigrant-rights activism has dangerously evolved from legal support into confrontational obstruction:

> "Our efforts were not aimed at interfering with authorities... What has reemerged during Trump's second term bears little resemblance to the rapid-response networks I once worked with. The emphasis is now on following and obstructing authorities, with an eye, at least for some participants, toward generating viral content." — [Compact](https://www.compactmag.com/r/1160b5b2?m=33b1996b-f427-4fb3-88a9-83a2095d7637)

The Emma Tucker/WSJ newsletter reported that even police who once backed ICE's mission are "losing faith in its tactics," with some law enforcement leaders voicing concerns that operations have "strayed from the administration's stated focus on public-safety threats."

[Semafor DC - Crunch Time](https://semafor.com/s/zrsmp2aIeN?email=wschenk@gmail.com) reported Vice President Vance visiting Minneapolis, calling the violence "the inevitable consequence of a state and local government that have decided that they're not going to cooperate with immigration enforcement at all."

### 2. Greenland Crisis and European Panic

**Coverage:** [Semafor Flagship](https://semafor.com/s/ygeXRa32Tf?email=wschenk@gmail.com), [Bloomberg Weekend](https://links.message.bloomberg.com/s/c/BuO-1MubV5TliQy1ZQnDAzK_WHGF8yLF-COPG66KQgwAbImGusZhYLqQGzTDyUTZlP6dS_bm7woB4_Vwon2HHqEEkggQmA_jAAxrTcRiCHGrOyaTyWfgx1fFlFZ1vPXsSZ4f3pNfpL1mkZjfEnh72HP2cVpo98_QY3R4fwn4feCxcEo4iJpHCGfSXt6qhOtu_GDplLymd3r6jFQN0RzD1aXLQOd3atiGGoPUV5Zbp1B8hFZ5zeT-Bow2K211gcPAUo3i1wQx9Ur4ZumfBJU64CxV59mkdg_O7X8WMQzXNJ5L5XWKikSe-Qpgoz2VZSMuUnVaX1hNyjDyAM8HNtPLkKHAQIkXEcN0WiUBXfvPg0jTEPOfqc3RcPML2g/4kKDqnAdWamMyBdanr25k5oTZ89xDfnJ/17), The Atlantic Daily, [Foreign Affairs This Week](https://link.foreignaffairs.com/view/64456883d447307be60958dcq0esq.eqrv/1897cc50), [Semafor DC - Crunch Time](https://semafor.com/s/zrsmp2aIeN?email=wschenk@gmail.com)

Trump's campaign to acquire Greenland through economic coercion dominated the week, with threats of tariffs and military action rattling European capitals before a vague "framework" was announced at Davos. The saga demonstrated what The Atlantic Daily called a fundamental change in Trump's behavior.

The Atlantic Daily analyzed Trump's second-term transformation: "The president's recent follow-through on his threats represents a real shift... During his first term, Trump was defined by his tendency to back down in any negotiation or fight."

> "There might be a conspicuous lack of details about the Greenland 'framework,' but it still moved markets... The TACO trade—or 'Trump Tamatigut Qunutittarpoq,' as they say in Greenland—is back." — [Bloomberg Weekend](https://links.message.bloomberg.com/s/c/BuO-1MubV5TliQy1ZQnDAzK_WHGF8yLF-COPG66KQgwAbImGusZhYLqQGzTDyUTZlP6dS_bm7woB4_Vwon2HHqEEkggQmA_jAAxrTcRiCHGrOyaTyWfgx1fFlFZ1vPXsSZ4f3pNfpL1mkZjfEnh72HP2cVpo98_QY3R4fwn4feCxcEo4iJpHCGfSXt6qhOtu_GDplLymd3r6jFQN0RzD1aXLQOd3atiGGoPUV5Zbp1B8hFZ5zeT-Bow2K211gcPAUo3i1wQx9Ur4ZumfBJU64CxV59mkdg_O7X8WMQzXNJ5L5XWKikSe-Qpgoz2VZSMuUnVaX1hNyjDyAM8HNtPLkKHAQIkXEcN0WiUBXfvPg0jTEPOfqc3RcPML2g/4kKDqnAdWamMyBdanr25k5oTZ89xDfnJ/17) (John Authers)

[Semafor Flagship](https://semafor.com/s/ygeXRa32Tf?email=wschenk@gmail.com) reported that Greenland's leaders "weren't consulted" on the framework Trump announced, with one official telling The Wall Street Journal they aren't "familiar" with it. European leaders emphasized the island's sovereignty is "not on the table," though Trump told Fox News the deal would give the US "total access."

[Foreign Affairs This Week](https://link.foreignaffairs.com/view/64456883d447307be60958dcq0esq.eqrv/1897cc50) featured analysis on "Trump's Year of Anarchy" and "The Unconstrained Presidency," warning about the erosion of American primacy.

### 4. 2026 Midterm Election Outlook

**Coverage:** Silver Bulletin

[Silver Bulletin](https://www.natesilver.net/p/midterm-polls-have-good-news-great) provided early analysis of the 2026 generic congressional ballot, finding Democrats leading by 5.3 points. Nate Silver noted that historically, the opposition party wins the House popular vote by 4.4 points on average in midterms.

> "Democrats currently lead by 5.3 points in our generic ballot average. That implies that everything from an 11.0-point Democratic win to a 0.4-point Republican edge is within the margin of error." — Silver Bulletin

The analysis suggests potential for a "blue tsunami" at the upper end of projections, which "would qualify as a blue tsunami... the number of Democratic seat gains would be somewhat curbed in the House because of extensive partisan gerrymandering. But that's the point at which even supposedly safe seats could break, potentially turning some gerrymanders into dummymanders."
