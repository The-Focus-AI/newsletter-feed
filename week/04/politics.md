# Week 04 Politics Newsletter Analysis (January 19-25, 2026)

## Overview

Week 04 marked a pivotal moment in global politics as Trump's territorial ambitions created unprecedented transatlantic tensions. [Semafor Flagship](https://semafor.com/s/g1f4tTMFHc?email=wschenk@gmail.com) and [Bloomberg Weekend](https://bloom.bg/3BMztSF) reported that Trump's threats to acquire Greenland through tariffs rattled markets and alliances alike, while [Citation Needed](https://www.citationneeded.news/issue-99/) exposed how cryptocurrency industry lobbyists have effectively "bought themselves a Congress." Domestically, [The Atlantic Daily](https://www.theatlantic.com/international/2026/01/kidnapped-baghdad/685470/) published Elizabeth Tsurkov's harrowing firsthand account of her kidnapping in Baghdad, offering rare insight into authoritarian brutality. Meanwhile, [ChinaTalk](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/zhipu-and-minimax-ipo) provided deep analysis of China's first two AI company IPOs, revealing the contours of Beijing's tech development strategy.

### 3. China's Tech IPOs and Industrial Policy Success

**Coverage:** [ChinaTalk](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/zhipu-and-minimax-ipo)

[ChinaTalk](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/zhipu-and-minimax-ipo) provided deep analysis of IPO prospectuses from Zhipu and MiniMax, the world's first two pure-play AI companies to go public. The documents offered rare transparency into China's AI business landscape and revealed how state backing and strategic partnerships drive the sector.

Key findings included Zhipu's heavy dependence on public-sector revenueâ€”13.6% from telecommunications SOEs alone, plus 29.4% from other government clients. With its "Tsinghua roots," Zhipu is described as "a state-fund darling." In contrast, MiniMax pursued a more commercial path, reporting over 212 million users across 200+ countries.

> "The impulse to constantly assert that the technology itself is the product is an interesting one. Both Zhipu and MiniMax are eager to describe themselves as foundation-model companies first, even if they have more specific application products that are clearly profitable." â€” [ChinaTalk](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/zhipu-and-minimax-ipo)

Both companies revealed dependence on diverse cloud computing suppliers, with MiniMax pursuing a "light-asset" strategy with no owned compute infrastructure. The prospectuses also showed diverging visions of AGI: Zhipu frames LLMs as the path through five stages ending in consciousness, while MiniMax focuses on multimodal intelligence.

A sobering finding: "The AI girlfriend business has razor-thin margins. The average Talkie/Xingye customer spent only US$5 in the first nine months of 2025."

### 4. Minneapolis Immigration Enforcement Crisis

**Coverage:** [Bloomberg Weekend](https://bloom.bg/3BMztSF), [Semafor Flagship](https://semafor.com/s/g1f4tTMFHc?email=wschenk@gmail.com)

The Trump administration moved to bolster law enforcement in Minneapolis, where ICE clashes with protesters have escalated following controversial shootings. An ICE agent shot a Minneapolis woman dead, which the White House called self-defense but local officials called murder. Two more people were wounded a day later.

[Bloomberg Weekend](https://bloom.bg/3BMztSF) reported the Pentagon ordered 1,500 US troops based in Alaska to prepare for potential deployment, and the FBI asked agents nationwide to volunteer for temporary duty in Minneapolis. A federal judge ordered ICE to pare back crowd control tactics, banning arrests and pepper-spraying of peaceful protesters.

> "It's going to take ICE a long time to dig out of this." â€” Former ICE director quoted in [Semafor Flagship](https://semafor.com/s/g1f4tTMFHc?email=wschenk@gmail.com)

[Semafor Flagship](https://semafor.com/s/g1f4tTMFHc?email=wschenk@gmail.com) noted that demonstrations have led to clashes, and 1,500 troops are on standby. A former ICE director warned that the agency's politicization is damaging relationships with law enforcement and city authorities.

### 7. Global Economic Realignment at Davos

**Coverage:** [Semafor Flagship](https://semafor.com/s/g1f4tTMFHc?email=wschenk@gmail.com), [Semafor Flagship (evening edition)](https://semafor.com/s/dBPKNuHOUH?email=wschenk@gmail.com), [Bloomberg Weekend](https://bloom.bg/3BMztSF)

The World Economic Forum at Davos became a stage for competing US and Chinese influence. [Semafor Flagship](https://semafor.com/s/dBPKNuHOUH?email=wschenk@gmail.com) reported that both Trump and Beijing's economic czar He Lifeng planned to convene CEOs. The US-China trade truce has given Western CEOs an opening to boost China ties, while Trump's disruptions allow Beijing to "present itself as a stable alternative to Washington."

[Semafor Flagship](https://semafor.com/s/g1f4tTMFHc?email=wschenk@gmail.com) noted that BlackRock CEO Larry Fink has "largely taken over" from founder Klaus Schwab, becoming "a quiet force reshaping the forum." Fink's leadership offers an opportunity to "reinvent a conference that critics argue has fallen out of kilter with global trends."

Economic optimism was surprisingly resilient: economists surveyed by The Wall Street Journal "increased their estimates for growth in both 2025 and 2026," while Goldman Sachs expressed "strongest conviction views for 2026 are our above-consensus GDP growth forecast and our below-consensus inflation forecast."

However, China presented a complex picture. While the economy "grew 5% last year, meeting Beijing's target," this masked "a widening gap in the country's two-track economy" with strong exports but "anemic domestic consumption and a faltering real estate sector." China's birthrate fell to its lowest level on record, and its population shrank for a fourth consecutive year.

## Cross-Newsletter Patterns

### Democratic Backsliding and Institutional Capture

Multiple newsletters documented how democratic institutions face pressure from concentrated wealth and power. [Citation Needed](https://www.citationneeded.news/issue-99/) showed the crypto industry's direct control over Congressional legislation ("when they've bought themselves a Congress, then they expect it to behave the way they say"), while [Semafor Media](https://semafor.com/s/25fQFddDEE?email=wschenk@gmail.com) revealed how media companies navigate political pressure from the White House. [The Atlantic](https://www.theatlantic.com/international/2026/01/kidnapped-baghdad/685470/) provided ground-level perspective on authoritarian brutality through Tsurkov's kidnapping account.

### US Isolationism Reshaping Global Order

Trump's Greenland threats ([Semafor Flagship](https://semafor.com/s/g1f4tTMFHc?email=wschenk@gmail.com), [Bloomberg Weekend](https://bloom.bg/3BMztSF)) and his confrontational approach to allies create openings for China to position itself as a stabilizing force ([Semafor Flagship evening](https://semafor.com/s/dBPKNuHOUH?email=wschenk@gmail.com)). Multiple sources noted European leaders beginning to treat "divorce" from the US as "inevitable" and Chinese officials "gleefully anticipating Europe's humiliation."

### Tech Industry's Dual Realities

[ChinaTalk](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/zhipu-and-minimax-ipo) showed Chinese AI companies achieving scale with state support and razor-thin consumer margins, while [Semafor Media](https://semafor.com/s/25fQFddDEE?email=wschenk@gmail.com) revealed OpenAI abandoning its anti-advertising stance due to "current technological limitations." Both point to the gap between AI hype and business reality.

### Wealth, Power, and Ethical Erosion

The week revealed multiple instances of wealthy actors skirting ethics rules: Todd Blanche's crypto conflicts ([Citation Needed](https://www.citationneeded.news/issue-99/)), Trump administration officials profiting from policy positions, and prediction market insider trading around the Maduro operation ([Citation Needed](https://www.citationneeded.news/issue-99/)). The pattern suggests growing acceptance of corruption at the highest levels.

### China's Alternative Development Model

[ChinaTalk](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/zhipu-and-minimax-ipo)'s IPO analysis, combined with [Semafor Flagship](https://semafor.com/s/g1f4tTMFHc?email=wschenk@gmail.com)'s economic coverage, revealed China's fundamentally different approach: heavy state involvement, focus on applications over infrastructure, light-asset strategies, and willingness to accept razor-thin margins in exchange for market penetration. Beijing's ability to use "centralized industrial policy" to "upend traditional notions about how innovation works" poses challenges to Western assumptions about economic development.
