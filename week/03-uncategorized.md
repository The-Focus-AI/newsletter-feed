# Week 03 Uncategorized Newsletter Analysis (January 12-18, 2026)

## Overview

This week's uncategorized newsletters span an unusually diverse range of topics, from mutual aid and wealth inequality to AI-driven software development paradigms and decentralized protocol governance. [Go Make Things](https://gomakethings.com/mutual-aid-and-asking-people-what-they-need/) delivered pointed social commentary on billionaire philanthropy and class solidarity, while [Geoffrey Huntley](https://ghuntley.com/r/a0e555fe?m=6d763223-fea6-4f7b-98af-864085a0f8a1) continued his provocative series on autonomous AI development methodologies. [Interconnected](https://interconnected.blog/r/5907b444?m=13ee6092-18b4-4710-96ca-c96c6fd588dd) offered a contrarian analysis of DeepSeek's advantage being precisely its lack of business model, while [Sentiers](https://sentiers.media/r/1ce11db3?m=a8fb9fc0-1be9-42e3-a8a8-f807098a7f6b) curated a thoughtful examination of the "post-American internet" and global polycrisis dynamics.

## Major Topics and Stories

### 1. AI-Driven Software Development and the "Ralph Loop" Methodology
**Coverage:** [Geoffrey Huntley](https://ghuntley.com/r/a0e555fe?m=6d763223-fea6-4f7b-98af-864085a0f8a1), [Geoffrey Huntley](https://ghuntley.com/r/10ca4a06?m=6d763223-fea6-4f7b-98af-864085a0f8a1), [Geoffrey Huntley](https://ghuntley.com/r/fb27d3d3?m=6d763223-fea6-4f7b-98af-864085a0f8a1)

Geoffrey Huntley published three interconnected pieces this week, all advancing his thesis that traditional "brick by brick" software development is dead, replaced by autonomous AI loops. His "Ralph Wiggum Technique" claims to reduce software development costs to under $10.42/hour. He announced becoming a "walking, talking financial instrument" through Solana-based creator tokens, receiving $300,000 within seven days from speculators betting on his AI research.

> "Software development is dead - I killed it. Software can now be developed cheaper than the wage of a burger flipper at maccas and it can be built autonomously whilst you are AFK." -- [Geoffrey Huntley](https://ghuntley.com/r/10ca4a06?m=6d763223-fea6-4f7b-98af-864085a0f8a1)

His follow-up on "back pressure" introduces a key concept for AI context engineering: designing just enough friction (tests, pre-commit hooks) to reject invalid AI generations without slowing the loop too much.

> "If you aren't capturing your back-pressure then you are failing as a software engineer." -- [Geoffrey Huntley](https://ghuntley.com/r/fb27d3d3?m=6d763223-fea6-4f7b-98af-864085a0f8a1)

### 2. Tech Industry Disruption: Tailwind Layoffs and AI's Impact
**Coverage:** [Go Make Things](https://gomakethings.com/when-the-tech-leopard-eats-your-face/)

Chris Ferdinandi reported on Tailwind CSS firing 75% of its engineering team due to AI's impact on their documentation-based business model. Despite Tailwind being "more popular than ever," revenue dropped 80% as users consult AI instead of documentation. Ferdinandi frames this as karmic, noting Tailwind's role in devaluing CSS expertise.

> "A new technology coming out -- one that he himself uses and enjoys -- and devaluing his life's work is less tragic and more poetic." -- [Go Make Things](https://gomakethings.com/when-the-tech-leopard-eats-your-face/)

The piece also notes Vercel and Google AI stepping in to sponsor Tailwind, which Ferdinandi characterizes as "fascist money" given Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch's political sympathies.

### 3. DeepSeek's No-Business-Model Advantage
**Coverage:** [Interconnected](https://interconnected.blog/r/5907b444?m=13ee6092-18b4-4710-96ca-c96c6fd588dd)

Kevin Xu argues that DeepSeek's enduring advantage is counterintuitive: having no business model at all. As the one-year anniversary of the "DeepSeek Moment" approaches, Xu notes that while DeepSeek's models are no longer the most capable, cheapest, or most open, founder Liang Wenfeng's self-funding from his quant fund allows total control over research direction without commercialization pressure.

> "The road to AGI requires compute, talent, and more than a healthy dose of good research taste. No one said it requires a business model." -- [Interconnected](https://interconnected.blog/r/5907b444?m=13ee6092-18b4-4710-96ca-c96c6fd588dd)

Xu contrasts this with xAI's recent $20 billion raise, noting even Elon Musk "couldn't say no to more money." He cites Ilya Sutskever's observation that transformative AI research (AlexNet, Transformers, ResNet) was done with remarkably little compute.

### 4. The Post-American Internet and Digital Sovereignty
**Coverage:** [Sentiers](https://sentiers.media/r/1ce11db3?m=a8fb9fc0-1be9-42e3-a8a8-f807098a7f6b)

Patrick Tanguay highlights Cory Doctorow's vision for building a "post-American internet" that reduces tech debt and unlocks monopoly wealth for global entrepreneurs. The piece identifies three vulnerability layers in current US-dominated internet architecture: legal (DMCA-style anticircumvention laws forced through trade agreements), physical (transoceanic cables tapped by NSA), and software (cloud services weaponized against institutions like the ICC).

> "Let's call time on enshittification. Let's seize the means of computation. Let's build the drop-in, free, open, auditable alternatives to the services and firmware we rely on." -- [Sentiers](https://sentiers.media/r/1ce11db3?m=a8fb9fc0-1be9-42e3-a8a8-f807098a7f6b) (quoting Doctorow)

[Sentiers](https://sentiers.media/r/1ce11db3?m=a8fb9fc0-1be9-42e3-a8a8-f807098a7f6b) also covers Bryan Alexander's polycrisis analysis, examining how overlapping crises -- demographic shifts, climate change, geopolitical tensions, technological developments -- interact and compound. Notably, the newsletter challenges the "sclerotic Europe" narrative by citing Gabriel Zucman's data showing European productivity per hour is comparable to the US but with lower carbon emissions, more leisure time, and greater equality.

### 5. Fediverse Protocol Governance and W3C Politics
**Coverage:** [Connected Places](https://connectedplaces.online/wp-admin/admin-ajax.php?action=tnptr&nltr=ODI7NjU4O2h0dHBzOi8vY29ubmVjdGVkcGxhY2VzLm9ubGluZS93cC1hZG1pbi9hZG1pbi1hamF4LnBocD9hY3Rpb249dG5wJm5hPXYmbms9NjU4LTNmNmU2NDBmZjImaWQ9ODI7O2Y5MjVkZDBhZThjMWNjMzI2YzM0MDEyYWY5ZTJiNTIy)

Laurens Hof provides a detailed analysis of the new W3C Social Web Working Group formed to maintain ActivityPub. The governance situation is "extremely funny" for a network aiming to be independent of Big Tech: only Meta and the Social Web Foundation are paid W3C members with standing to participate, while Mastodon, Framasoft, and other fediverse builders are excluded unless invited.

> "Using network architecture to solve issues of power distribution simply shifts bottlenecks rather than eliminating them. A decentralised protocol does not automatically produce decentralised governance." -- [Connected Places](https://connectedplaces.online/wp-admin/admin-ajax.php?action=tnptr&nltr=ODI7NjU4O2h0dHBzOi8vY29ubmVjdGVkcGxhY2VzLm9ubGluZS93cC1hZG1pbi9hZG1pbi1hamF4LnBocD9hY3Rpb249dG5wJm5hPXYmbms9NjU4LTNmNmU2NDBmZjImaWQ9ODI7O2Y5MjVkZDBhZThjMWNjMzI2YzM0MDEyYWY5ZTJiNTIy)

### 6. Wealth Inequality and Class Solidarity
**Coverage:** [Go Make Things](https://gomakethings.com/mutual-aid-and-asking-people-what-they-need/), [Go Make Things](https://gomakethings.com/who-stole-your-cookie/)

Chris Ferdinandi published two pieces on social justice themes. The first recounts hands-on mutual aid work with homeless individuals, emphasizing the importance of asking people what they need rather than assuming. The second offers a pointed critique of billionaire philanthropy using Taylor Swift as an example.

> "It would take you 10 years to make $1 million. It would take you 10,000 years to make $1 billion. Billionaires should not exist." -- [Go Make Things](https://gomakethings.com/who-stole-your-cookie/)

> "When the rich do philanthropy, it's brand sanitization. It's PR." -- [Go Make Things](https://gomakethings.com/who-stole-your-cookie/)

### 7. U.S. Intervention in Venezuela
**Coverage:** [Everything is Political](content/uncategorized/everything-is-political/2026-01-13-everything-is-political-we-are-not-living-in-unprecedented-t.md), [Everything is Political](content/uncategorized/everything-is-political/2026-01-15-everything-is-political-sanctions-are-a-tool-of-empire.md)

Everything is Political published two pieces on U.S. military action in Venezuela, including the abduction of President Maduro. The newsletter frames this within historical patterns of American interventionism and argues that sanctions primarily harm ordinary people rather than governments.

### 8. Go Programming Language Updates
**Coverage:** [Golang Weekly](content/uncategorized/golang-weekly/2026-01-16-go-1-26-rc2-go-1-25-6-and-go-1-24-12-released.md)

Golang Weekly announced Go 1.26 RC2 with security fixes in archive/zip and Request.ParseForm. Notable technical content includes discussions of atomic operations vs. mutexes, implementing mutexes from scratch, and a Go runtime for the Sega Dreamcast. The issue also notes that "even Linus Torvalds is 'vibe coding' now" using Google's Antigravity tool.

## Cross-Newsletter Patterns

**AI's Disruptive Labor Impact**: Both [Geoffrey Huntley](https://ghuntley.com/r/10ca4a06?m=6d763223-fea6-4f7b-98af-864085a0f8a1) and [Go Make Things](https://gomakethings.com/when-the-tech-leopard-eats-your-face/) address AI's impact on tech employment, though from opposite perspectives. Huntley celebrates autonomous development as inevitable progress; Ferdinandi highlights its human cost through the Tailwind layoffs.

**Governance and Power Asymmetries**: [Connected Places](https://connectedplaces.online/wp-admin/admin-ajax.php?action=tnptr&nltr=ODI7NjU4O2h0dHBzOi8vY29ubmVjdGVkcGxhY2VzLm9ubGluZS93cC1hZG1pbi9hZG1pbi1hamF4LnBocD9hY3Rpb249dG5wJm5hPXYmbms9NjU4LTNmNmU2NDBmZjImaWQ9ODI7O2Y5MjVkZDBhZThjMWNjMzI2YzM0MDEyYWY5ZTJiNTIy) and [Sentiers](https://sentiers.media/r/1ce11db3?m=a8fb9fc0-1be9-42e3-a8a8-f807098a7f6b) both examine how decentralized systems still concentrate power in unexpected places -- W3C membership fees and US trade agreements respectively.

**Alternative Funding Models**: [Geoffrey Huntley](https://ghuntley.com/r/a0e555fe?m=6d763223-fea6-4f7b-98af-864085a0f8a1) and [Interconnected](https://interconnected.blog/r/5907b444?m=13ee6092-18b4-4710-96ca-c96c6fd588dd) both highlight unconventional funding approaches. Huntley embraces Solana-based creator tokens; Xu praises DeepSeek's self-funding from quant fund profits. Both argue these approaches enable purer research focus than venture capital.

**Digital Sovereignty Themes**: Both [Sentiers](https://sentiers.media/r/1ce11db3?m=a8fb9fc0-1be9-42e3-a8a8-f807098a7f6b) and [Connected Places](https://connectedplaces.online/wp-admin/admin-ajax.php?action=tnptr&nltr=ODI7NjU4O2h0dHBzOi8vY29ubmVjdGVkcGxhY2VzLm9ubGluZS93cC1hZG1pbi9hZG1pbi1hamF4LnBocD9hY3Rpb249dG5wJm5hPXYmbms9NjU4LTNmNmU2NDBmZjImaWQ9ODI7O2Y5MjVkZDBhZThjMWNjMzI2YzM0MDEyYWY5ZTJiNTIy) grapple with questions of who controls critical digital infrastructure. Doctorow envisions a post-American internet built on open-source commons; the Fediverse Report reveals how formal governance structures can inadvertently give Big Tech power in supposedly decentralized systems.
